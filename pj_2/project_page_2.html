How is public transportation connected to climate change in our daily lives?

The function of passenger transport can be divided up into public and private transport. The latter of which is the most common method of transportation for people to use in Germany. Typically, private transportation means taking a car when and wherever you need to go. This comes with a big impact on the environment.

Impact transportation has on CO2 emissions

Why is public transport more enviromentally friendly than individual transport?

Three benefits of using Public Transport over individual Transport include the reduction of carbon emissions, air pollution and congestion on roads. Public transport brings these benefits to the table even though it may still rely on combustion engines, because more people can be transported using the same amount of space, which in turn reduces the amount of emissions produced to transport one person. Of our global emissions, 29% can be directly attributed to transport, which means that if society were to change commuting habits and increase its use of public transport, emissions can be reduced annually by 37 million tons according to the C2ES.

What impact would changing to public transport for your daily commute have ?

If you would take public transport to work instead of your own car, the German “Umwelt Bundesamt” suggests that you would save about 3.700€ and about 320KG per year for a travel distance of 25 kilometers (Assumption: on 220 days, 25 km by suburban train and on foot instead of by car to work). In addition to saving a significant amount of money (especially taking into account the current fuel prices) you also get to utilise the time to work, read or to do anything you wish.

Now seeing that public transport does have many advantages when looking at how environmentally friendly it is in comparison to cars, you can ask yourself why it is not the go to option for many people to travel from a to b.
