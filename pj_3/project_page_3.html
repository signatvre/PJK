Avoiding traffic, saving time looking for a parking space and helping the environment – using public transportation has many advantages. Yet the car remains dominant, especially in Germany. In 2020 for example, the share of public transportation in motorized passenger transport was only 11%. That is below the EU average of 14% and far behind the leading countries Hungary and Austria with 23% and 26%.

Why is public transport so rarely used ?

According to the demographic portal of the federal and state governments, 70 million people live less than 600 meters from the next bus stop. However, that does not mean that public transportation is constantly available. It often means only one bus per hour. And that only between 7:00 and 17:00. Outside of these times and on the weekends, the situation gets much worse.

Additionally, there is a huge difference between the cities and the rural areas. In sparsely populated counties, 40% of the people do not have access to decent public transportation. That leads to public transportation being responsible for a mere 5-7% of the total traffic. Hence, the majority depends on cars and currently, that does not seem to be about to change.

Our mission – making public transport more attractive

We believe in the potential of public transportation. But at the same time, we know that the current system would need to be changed for it to become more attractive. In order to achieve that, we have come up with several ideas that could be realised in Germany.

How to make public transport more attractive 101

Expansion of public transportation
First of all, we propose an increase in the frequency of busses and trains – including evenings and weekends. We believe that public transportation has to meet the needs of the people. That is why the regular public transportation schedule should be complimented by special services, such as extra school trains.

Making public transportation modern and comfortable
Many bus and train stops are old and run down. That needs to change with new and durable seats, roofings etc. There should also be possiblities to safely park your car or bicycle at your local train or bus stop. Furthermore, we think it is important to replace old train wagons and busses with new models. Since the world is becoming more and more digital, Wifi should be available in and around public transportation.

Ensuring the safety of passengers
Especially late at night or during the dark winter months, many people are concerned about their safety at bus and train stops. These concerns have to be countered with an effective security concept. This should include visible cameras and lighting. We also believe that it is important to train drivers and other personell on how to react to different situations.

Accessibility for everyone
If we want everyone to use public transportation, it has to be accessible for everyone as well. Especially handicapped persons, persons of age or parents with small children have to face a lot of challenges when using public transport. This could be improved by e. g. installing ramps and other aids for people with vehicles like wheelchairs or strollers. Essential information concerning departure times, schedules and cancellations have to be provided through various mediums, especially online.
