These shifts may be natural, but since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels (like coal, oil and gas), which produces heat-trapping gases. “

Recently, this process has been accelerating and consequently endangering our environment and its ecosystems. Furthermore, a slight increase in temperatures globally leads to e. g. the melting of the polar caps and stark changes in weather patterns, such as an increase regarding hurricanes and floods – global warming is therefore not something that can be ignored any longer.

Germany's goals regarding climate protection

2030: reduction of greenhouse gas emissions by 65% compared to 1990

2040: reduction of greenhouse gas emissions by 88% compared to 1990

2045: emission neutrality

2050: negative emissions

Measures taken to achieve Germany's climate goals

Germany has taken a broad range of measures in order to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and therefore contain the development of climate change. So far, the implementation of the listed programmes and more has reduced Germany’s greenhouse gas emissions by about 40% since 1990.

The central measures include:

• Emissions trading / pricing the emission of carbon regarding the warmth and transport sectors

• Investing in energy efficient and sustainable programs to minimise emissions, especially in the sectors of industry, buildings, and energy industry

• Substituting the purchase of climate-friendly heating systems and electric cars

• Expansion of renewable energies, especially wind power

• Carbon sequestration by natural resources, e. g. forests

• Creating jobs to actively support structural change
